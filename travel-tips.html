General

Travel Tips

Visa & Entry Requirements

Many nationalities (including the US, UK, EU, and Canada) do not require a visa for short tourist stays in Panama. Travelers are typically granted entry for up to 90–180 days. You may be asked to show proof of onward travel and sufficient funds. Passport validity of at least 6 months is recommended. Please check your specific country’s requirements in advance.

Getting Around

Uber and private transportation are widely available, reliable, and generally affordable throughout Panama City. Traffic can be heavy during peak hours, so allow extra time when heading to events or reservations.

What to Wear

Panama has a warm, tropical climate year-round, with high humidity and temperatures typically ranging between 24–32°C (75–90°F). Dress in light, breathable fabrics such as linen and cotton to stay comfortable. Flowy outfits, sandals, and comfortable walking shoes are ideal for daytime exploring. Don’t forget sunglasses, sunscreen, and a light layer for air-conditioned indoor spaces.

Dining Reservations

Panama City has a vibrant dining scene, and many of the top restaurants fill up quickly—especially on weekends. We highly recommend making reservations in advance for fine dining and popular rooftop spots.

Booking Experiences

Island tours, yacht charters, and canal experiences are in high demand. Booking ahead (particularly for weekends or group outings) ensures availability and smoother planning.

Currency & Payments

Panama uses both the Panamanian Balboa and the US Dollar (1:1), and USD is accepted everywhere. Credit cards are widely used, but it’s helpful to carry small bills for taxis, tips, and local vendors.

Language

Spanish is the official language, but English is commonly spoken in hotels, restaurants, and tourist areas—so getting around is easy for international guests.

Safety

Panama City is generally safe, especially in well-known areas like Casco Antiguo, Punta Pacifica, and San Francisco. As with any major city, it’s best to stay aware of your surroundings and avoid carrying valuables unnecessarily.

Tipping Etiquette

A service charge is often included in restaurant bills, but additional tipping (10%–20%) for excellent service is appreciated. Tipping drivers, hotel staff, and guides is also customary.
